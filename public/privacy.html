Privacy

EA Watch — personal assistant and contact experience. This page summarizes how the app handles data in the current implementation.

EA chat

Ordinary conversation with EA is not saved as a full transcript in our database. The browser keeps a short in-memory history for the current tab so follow-up messages can include recent context; reloading the page clears that history.

What we store (database)

We use a SQLite database (Turso) for accounts, devices, sessions, settings, tasks, routines, and logs. Examples include:

  • action_log — structured events for some flows (e.g. routine picker, task actions from chat, weather/news/briefing chips, routine steps). Entries include metadata, not a dump of every chat message.
  • owner_config — your settings, briefing preferences, contact-card fields, etc.
  • sessions — session tokens (cookie-based auth), tied to your device.

Third-party services

Message content is sent to Anthropic (Claude) when you use EA chat and related features. How Anthropic processes and retains data is governed by their policies. Other optional services (e.g. news, geocoding, Google Places for Interest radar) receive only what is needed for that request.

Contact changes

This app may evolve. Check this page and the repo README from time to time. For questions, contact the operator of your deployment.
